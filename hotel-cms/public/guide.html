Getting Started

Log in, explore your dashboard, and understand your hotel portfolio at a glance.

Logging In

Open hotelCMS in your browser and sign in with your email and password. If your organization uses single sign-on, you'll see that option on the login page too.

Login Screen

First Time? Create Your Organization

If you're setting up hotelCMS for the first time, you'll be asked to create your organization. Enter your company name and you're in. You'll automatically become the Admin for your organization.

  1. Enter your organization name — this is the name of your hotel group or management company.
  2. Set up your first hotel — you'll add a name, category, and basic contact details.
  3. Explore the dashboard — you'll land on a bird's-eye view of all your properties.

Your Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is your command center. At the top, you'll see summary cards showing your total hotels, pages, and overall health scores. Below that, each hotel is listed with its current status.

Dashboard Overview

Action Queue — Your To-Do List

The action queue surfaces the most important tasks across all your properties. Missing meta descriptions, incomplete pages, broken links — they all appear here, sorted by priority. Think of it as your daily checklist.

💡

Check your action queue first thing each morning. It's the fastest way to keep all your hotel websites in great shape.

Managing Hotels

Add properties, configure their details, and set up everything guests need to find and contact you.

Creating a New Hotel

Click the "Add Hotel" button on your dashboard. Fill in the basics:

  1. Hotel name — the full property name as you want it displayed to guests.
  2. Category — boutique, resort, business, heritage, or another type. This helps AI tailor content to your property style.
  3. Contact info — phone number, email address, and physical address.
Add Hotel Form

Hotel Detail Page

Click any hotel from the dashboard to open its detail page. This is the main workspace for that property. You'll see tabs for Pages, SEO, Smart Links, Theme, Languages, Competitors, and Reviews.

Editing Hotel Info

Need to update the phone number or change the category? Open the hotel detail page and click the edit icon next to the hotel name. Make your changes and save.

Setting Up Smart Links

Smart Links are the key actions guests can take from your website. Set these up before building pages — they get used everywhere automatically.

Configuring a Theme

Your theme controls how your website looks and feels. You can customize:

  • Colors — primary brand color, secondary color, background tones

Building Pages

The visual page builder lets you create beautiful, conversion-ready pages without writing a single line of code.

Adding Pages to Your Hotel

From the hotel detail page, click "Add Page". Give it a name (like "Homepage", "Rooms", or "Dining") and a URL slug. The page is created as a draft — you can build it out before publishing.

Opening the Page Builder

Click on any page to enter the page builder. On the left, you'll see your page sections stacked vertically. On the right, a live preview shows exactly what guests will see.

Page Builder — Visual Editor

Adding Sections

Click the "+ Add Section" button to open the component picker. Choose from 12 ready-to-use section types:

Editing Section Content

Select any section to open the props editor on the right. This is where you change headlines, descriptions, images, button text, and other content specific to that section type. Every change is reflected in the live preview instantly.

Reordering Sections

Use the up/down arrows on each section to reorder them. Drag a hero banner to the top, move testimonials right above your call to action — arrange the page however tells the best story.

Showing & Hiding Sections

Don't want to delete a section but need it off the page temporarily? Toggle the visibility icon to hide it. Hidden sections are saved but won't appear on the live website.

Deleting Sections

Click the trash icon on any section to remove it. You'll be asked to confirm before it's deleted.

Live Preview

The right side of the page builder shows a real-time preview of your page. What you see is exactly what your guests will see. Resize your browser to check how it looks on different screen sizes.

💡

Use the full-screen preview button to see your page without the editor sidebar. Great for a final review before publishing.

Using AI

Your AI assistant understands hospitality. Just tell it what you want and watch the magic happen.

The Command Bar

Press ⌘K (Mac) or Ctrl+K (Windows) anywhere in the page builder to open the AI command bar. Type what you want in plain English — no special syntax needed.

AI Command Bar

What You Can Ask AI to Do

Here are some examples of what works. Try them out — you can always undo changes.

⌘K "Make the headline more inviting" Text change
⌘K "Make the hero background dark blue" Visual change
⌘K "Redesign the hero with a cinematic layout" Component change
⌘K "Add a gallery section after the hero" Structural change
⌘K "Build a homepage inspired by hablis.com" URL reference

How AI Works Behind the Scenes

When you give AI a command, here's what happens:

  1. Analyze — AI reads your instruction and figures out the best approach.
  2. Review tier & cost — it picks the most efficient route, from simple text edits to full redesigns.
  3. Execute — the change is made to your page sections.
  4. See changes — the live preview updates instantly so you can review.

The Logs Panel

Curious what AI is doing? Open the logs panel at the bottom of the page builder. It shows a real-time feed of AI's thinking process — what it analyzed, what decisions it made, and what it changed.

The Diff Panel

After any AI change, the diff panel shows you a clear before-and-after comparison. Green highlights show what was added, red shows what was removed. This way, you always know exactly what changed.

AI Diff View — Before & After

AI Remembers Your Preferences

The more you use AI, the better it understands your brand voice and style. If you consistently ask for "warm, inviting" language, AI will learn to match that tone across future suggestions.

AI Direct Code Editing

For pixel-perfect customizations that go beyond standard components, AI can inject custom CSS or replace entire sections with custom HTML. Just describe what you want — "make the hero full-width with a gradient overlay and centered white text" — and AI writes the code.

🎯

Be specific with your instructions. Instead of "make it better," try "make the headline shorter and add urgency for a summer promotion." The more detail you give, the better the result.

Multi-Language

Reach international guests with professionally translated content in 18 languages.

Adding a New Language

From the hotel detail page, go to the Languages tab and click "Add Language". Select from 18 supported languages. Once added, AI will automatically translate all your existing pages into the new language.

Language Management

How Translation Works

When you add a language, AI translates every page section — headlines, descriptions, button text, everything. It doesn't just do a word-for-word translation; it adapts the tone and phrasing to feel natural in each language.

Switching Between Languages in the CMS

In the page builder, use the language switcher dropdown at the top to toggle between languages. This lets you review and edit the translated version of each page.

How Guests See Different Languages

On your live website, guests see a language switcher icon. Clicking it lets them choose their preferred language. The entire site — all pages, all sections — switches seamlessly.

Editing Translated Content

AI translations are a starting point. You can manually edit any translated text using the same props editor you use for the original language. Just switch to the language you want to edit and make your changes.

💡

Have a native speaker review AI translations for important markets. While AI translations are high quality, a local touch for your top markets can make a real difference.

SEO

Help potential guests find your hotel when they search online.

What SEO Means for Your Hotel

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) determines how easily people find your hotel when they search on Google. Good SEO means more visibility, more website visitors, and ultimately more bookings — all without paying for ads.

Your SEO Score

Each hotel page gets an SEO score that measures:

  • Page titles & descriptions — are they set and the right length?
  • Headings structure — is your content well organized?
  • Image alt text — can search engines understand your images?
  • Content quality — is there enough meaningful text?
  • Mobile friendliness — does it work well on phones?
SEO Audit Dashboard

Common Issues and How to Fix Them

The SEO panel lists issues sorted by impact. Click any issue to see a clear explanation of what's wrong and how to fix it. Many fixes are as simple as adding a missing description or updating a page title.

Auto-Generated Sitemap & Structured Data

hotelCMS automatically creates a sitemap (a map of all your pages for search engines) and adds structured data (extra information that helps Google show rich results like star ratings and prices). You don't need to do anything — it just works.

ℹ️

SEO improvements take time. After making changes, it typically takes 2-4 weeks for Google to re-index your pages and for ranking improvements to appear.

Campaigns NEW

Run promotions across all your properties at once. One campaign, every hotel updated with AI-tailored content.

What Are Campaigns?

Campaigns let you push a promotion, seasonal offer, or brand initiative to multiple hotel websites simultaneously. Instead of manually updating each property, you create one campaign and AI adapts the content for each hotel — a boutique beach resort gets different messaging than a downtown business hotel, even though it's the same promotion.

Creating a Campaign

  1. Set a title — for example, "Summer Escape 2026" or "Holiday Season Special".
  2. Write a brief — describe what the promotion is about, key selling points, dates, and any specific messaging you want.
  3. Select hotels — choose which properties should participate. You can select all or pick specific ones.
Campaign Builder

Launching a Campaign

When you launch, AI takes your brief and creates tailored content for each selected hotel. The content is adapted to each property's style, category, and audience. You can monitor the deployment status for each hotel on the campaign dashboard.

Checking Deployment Status

The campaign dashboard shows the deployment status for each hotel: pending, deployed, or failed. If something goes wrong, you'll see a clear error message and can retry.

Ending a Campaign

When the promotion is over, click "End Campaign". This removes the campaign content from all participating hotel websites in one step. No manual cleanup needed.

⚠️

Ending a campaign immediately removes the promotional content from all hotel websites. Make sure the promotion period is actually over before ending it.

Competitive Intelligence NEW

Monitor competitor hotel websites and get AI-powered insights to stay ahead.

Why Track Competitors?

Knowing what your competitors are doing with their websites — new promotions, pricing changes, content updates — helps you stay competitive. hotelCMS monitors their sites for you and suggests strategic counter-moves.

Adding a Competitor

  1. Enter the competitor name — the hotel or brand you want to track.
  2. Paste their website URL — the main page of their hotel website.
  3. Assign to a hotel — link the competitor to one of your properties for targeted analysis.
Competitor Dashboard

Scanning Competitors

Click "Scan" on any competitor to fetch their latest website content. You can also scan all competitors at once. The system extracts their content, compares it to your hotel, and generates AI insights.

Understanding Insights

After each scan, AI generates competitive insights:

  • What they changed — new promotions, updated pricing, or content shifts
  • What they do well — areas where the competitor's site outperforms yours
  • Recommended counter-moves — specific actions you can take to stay competitive

Tracking Changes Over Time

Every scan is saved in the competitor's history. Compare snapshots over time to spot trends, like when a competitor runs seasonal promotions or changes their pricing strategy.

💡

Scan your top competitors weekly. The more data points you have, the better AI can identify patterns and suggest strategic responses.

Guest Review Responses NEW

AI drafts on-brand responses to guest reviews. Copy, paste, done.

Why Respond to Reviews?

Responding to guest reviews shows potential guests that you care. It improves your reputation on platforms like Google, TripAdvisor, and Booking.com, and can directly influence booking decisions. hotelCMS makes it fast with AI-generated responses.

Adding a Review

  1. Paste the guest's name — as it appears on the review platform.
  2. Select the platform — Google, TripAdvisor, Booking.com, or others.
  3. Set the rating — the star rating the guest gave (1-5).
  4. Paste the review text — copy the full review from the platform.
Guest Review Dashboard

How AI Responds

As soon as you add a review, AI analyzes the sentiment and drafts an appropriate response:

Positive reviews get warm, grateful acknowledgment
Neutral reviews get friendly engagement
Negative reviews get empathetic, resolution-focused replies

The response is written in your hotel's brand voice — the same tone AI uses across your website content.

Copy to Clipboard

Happy with the AI response? Click "Copy" to copy it to your clipboard, then paste it directly into the review platform. Need a different take? Click "Regenerate" for an alternative wording.

Review Analytics

The review dashboard shows aggregate statistics for each hotel: average rating, total reviews, sentiment breakdown (positive/neutral/negative), and response rate. Use this to track your hotel's online reputation over time.

ℹ️

Aim for a 100% response rate. Responding to every review — positive and negative — shows guests and Google that you're actively engaged.

Brand Governance NEW

Maintain brand consistency across every hotel in your portfolio. Set it once, enforce it everywhere.

What Is Brand Governance?

When you manage multiple hotels, keeping a consistent brand across all of them is challenging. Brand governance lets you define organization-wide rules for how your hotels look and what content stays the same everywhere.

Organization-Level Theme

Set brand colors, typography, and spacing at the organization level. These override individual hotel themes for the values you specify. Hotels can still customize their own non-locked theme values, but the brand-level settings always take precedence.

Brand Theme Editor

Locked Sections

Some content must be the same across every property — legal disclaimers, brand headers, footer links, compliance notices. Mark these sections as "locked" and they'll cascade to all hotels automatically.

  • Editors cannot modify locked sections
  • AI commands targeting locked sections are blocked automatically
  • Only organization admins can update locked content

AI Protection

If someone tries to use the AI command bar to modify a locked section — for example, "change the footer text" when the footer is locked — AI will politely explain that the section is brand-locked and suggest contacting an admin instead.

🎯

Start with locking your footer and legal sections. These are the most common sections that need to be consistent across all properties. You can lock additional sections over time.

Media Library

Upload, manage, and optimize all your hotel imagery in one place.

Uploading Images

Open the media library from the sidebar or from within the page builder. Drag and drop images, or click "Upload" to browse your computer. You can upload multiple images at once.

Media Library

How Images Are Processed

Every image you upload is automatically optimized for the web. hotelCMS:

  • Strips EXIF data (location, camera info) for privacy
  • Converts to modern formats (WebP, AVIF) for faster loading
  • Creates multiple sizes for different devices (responsive srcset)
  • Generates AI alt-text for accessibility and SEO

You don't need to resize or compress images before uploading. Just upload the highest quality version you have.

Editing Alt Text

Alt text describes your image for search engines and visually impaired users. Click any image in the library to edit its alt text. AI generates a starting point, but you can customize it. Be descriptive: "Sunset view from the rooftop pool" is much better than "image1".

Deleting Images

Select an image and click "Delete". If the image is currently used on a page, you'll see a warning before it's removed.

ℹ️

Upload images at their original resolution. The system handles optimization automatically, so you always get the best quality at the smallest file size.

Version History

Every change is saved. Review, compare, and roll back at any time.

Every Change Is Tracked

Every time you (or AI) edits a page, a new version is automatically saved. Nothing is ever lost. You can always go back to any previous version. hotelCMS uses RFC 6902 patches for efficient storage — only the changes are stored, not full copies.

Version History Timeline

Viewing Version History

Open any page in the builder and click the version history icon. You'll see a timeline of every change, with timestamps and descriptions of what was modified. Click any version to preview it.

Publishing Drafts

New changes are saved as drafts until you're ready to publish. This means you can make edits, review them, and only push them live when everything looks perfect. Click "Publish" to make your current draft the live version.

Rolling Back to a Previous Version

Made a change you regret? Find the version you want in the history timeline and click "Restore". The page will revert to that exact state. The restored version becomes your new draft, so you still need to publish it to go live.

Smart Merge

If you make manual CMS edits while AI has custom code overrides on the same section, hotelCMS detects the conflict and gives you options: keep your changes, discard them, or re-apply the AI overrides. This prevents any work from being silently lost.

💡

Don't be afraid to experiment. Since every change is tracked, you can always roll back. Try bold changes — you have a safety net.
